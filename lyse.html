Q&A with Lyse Aneze

A tech enthusiast exploring the integration of web development and human centred design. I am from Kigali, Rwanda

Where have you studied before coming to ALU?

I attended a high school in Kigali known as Excella School

How did you hear about ALU?

I discovered ALU through an event organised to introduce the university to high school students not long after its inauguration.

Why you wanted to study at ALU?

Through the informative event, I learnt about the unique ALU model and its mission to use entrepreneurship as a tool to solve community problems in Africa. I knew I would strive in the student-focused learning techniques because my intellectual curiosity is sparked only through active learning.

Moving to AlU to begin studying

The fact that we received an email in less than a week to travel to Mauritius caught my family and I offguard. I had to shop, prepare documents, test positive for multiple COVID tests while having to move around the city, and make sure to not forget anything. It was stressful to say the least but everything went according to plan and the night we landed in Mauritius, we were driven to our quarantine hotel and that marked the start of a scary but exciting college journey

The Application process and being accepted

I applied to ALC for the second intake for the class of 2024. It took me a little bit of meticulousness to submit a smooth application but I managed to do so way ahead of the deadline and received my admissions decision about 2 months later.

Moving to ALU to begin studying.

The fact that we received an email in less than a week to travel to Mauritius caught my family and I offguard. I had to shop, prepare documents, test positive for multiple COVID tests while having to move around the city, and make sure to not forget anything. It was stressful to say the least but everything went according to plan and the night we landed in Mauritius, we were driven to our quarantine hotel and that marked the start of a scary but exciting college journey.

Meeting other students..

I tend to be more introverted so I was intrigued by the idea of making friends from all over the continent but anxious about breaking the barrier of the awkward phase where people are just getting to know each. Quickly, I made quite a number of acquaintances, few of whom turned into friendships in the long run.

About the classes that you have been studying, including examples of work.

We have gained massive knowledge but that which marks me the most is during our Leadeship core. We learnt some data analysis skills that can be applied to many fields, how communicate through writing and orally, and project management. These lessons set a great stepping stone for our various chosen majors that we began to study in our second year here.

Advice to prospective students.

Always stay true to who are and who you want to become but also allowing yourself to grow. Do not confuse the thoughts and ideas of your friends for you own. You personality is what makes you unique and will be challenged in a new environment.

A resume of your time at ALU so far.

ALC has definitely been a mix emotions from struggling with interpersonal relationships to making local friends. Adjusting to heavy workload. Exploring the island finally once all COVID-19 restrictions were removed. I faced challenges in all areas of my life and handling was hard being away from home but we are entering a new phase where we get to study and dive deeper into the mauritian experience. Looking forward to improve my academic and social life the most.

Ideas for the future & careers, for each student.

I have great interest in web technologies so I see myself starting an agency that develops digital homes for organisations with a human-centred approach to the design and development of those websites and social media platforms.
