Q&A with Samatha Kelosi

I am passionate about using technology to raise awareness of global warming and its detrimental impacts on the environment.

Where have you studied before coming to ALU?

I attended a single-sex boys school for my Advanced Level Studies. Well, it did have girls at A level of course! After which I took a whole gap year, working while waiting for my transition to university.

How did you hear about ALU?

During my time in A Level, I attended a lot of open day ceremonies with several schools and workshops for various overseas universities so much that I knew most of the good schools in and outside Africa. ALU was part of the list!

Moving to ALU to begin studying.

I had a lot of separation anxiety as a child, afraid of copying with new places or environments. But to my surprise, moving to a whole different school, in a different country with complete strangers was part of the bravest decisions I have ever made in my life. My first few days in Mauritius were not so tourist-friendly as I had to stay locked up in a hotel to quarantine as the Covid situation was a bit intense. However, the moving process and preparations were all enjoyable for me as most people back home were trying to have the best last moments with me and it was a pretty nice farewell.ocuments and retook medical tests

Meeting other students..

The excitement and rush of getting to know who is who behind their social media pictures was absolutely fun for me. It’s quite a small school, it took me almost a month to get to know everyone in my year group.

About the classes that you have been studying, including examples of work.

Funny enough, my biggest lesson in university is that students will always do an assignment the night before it’s due. Battling between meeting up with deadlines, trying to make it to class with a heavy hangover and also trying to balance a social life is a real sport in university. My favourite courses have been Web Development and Software Engineering.

Advice to prospective students.

Life is for the living!

A resume of your time at ALU so far.

With the assistance from the Student Ventures Program at ALU, Ihave managed to launch two of my startups as well as intern with reputable organisations and establish a good connection in the Mauritius Business Network.

Social Life – all students to contribute together.

Living on campus gave me an opportunity to interact within my neighbors, having room commons which mainly joined us for assignments, football matches, and cooking was my key take anyway.

Ideas for the future & careers, for each student.

I hope to expand my entrepreneurial projects as well as qualify to work in the IT sector as a Software Engineer
