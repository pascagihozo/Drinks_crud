Q&A with Bridget Chirimbani

I am passionate about technology in the health field. I hope to use AI for the benefit of communities to improve their health hence their quality of life. With a lot going on already , my goal is to improve on what is on the ground already whilst working on new methods that could benefit people from all walks of life.

Where have you studied before coming to ALU?

Before coming to ALU I carried out my studies in Zimbabwe at a missionary school called Monte Csssino.

How did you hear about ALU?

I heard about ALU through social media. It was titled “The Harvard of Africa”, a very inticing topic that immediately caught my interest and I decided to delve deeper into the place.

Why you wanted to study at ALU?

ALU unlike many universities focuses on the student’s passion and how they can use it to improve their community and make a living. With accredited professors and many programs outside of the norm class convention,ALU was a dream I wanted to become a part of.

How you heard about ALU.

My first time to hear ALU was when ALU students came to visit our school, in 2018. It was like marketing team because they came to syntehsize us to apply in ALU after High School later my brother interned in ALU so I started loving the University

The Application process and being accepted

My application process, apart from the terrible internet connection back at home , went smoothly, it took me less than a month to receive my admission decision.

Moving to ALU to begin studying.

It was particularly difficult for me to move to the island as these were the horrifying times of Covid 19. Not only that but the VISA application process was ardous and stressful and the wait was no better. Luckily I got accepted without having to resubmit any documents. Prices for COVID tests had plummeted as a strategy to stop people from traveling and this caused a strain on my family .

Meeting other students..

Getting into the island , I tested positive for covid and had to isolate for more than 6 weeks. I missed orientation and the first weeks of class. I had no friends on campus and the time I was then taken to campus was a full blow for me as people had already made friends and groups. I met my roommate first and gradually from the classroom and cafeteria , started to meet other people.

About the classes that you have been studying, including examples of work.

I started off with Leadership Core which was a refreshing, informative and interactive course. Moving on to my degree choice, Computing, I found the transition rather challenging as the classes like Software Engineering and Mathematics were more demanding but with time I got the hang of it.

Advice to prospective students.

To come with an open mind, be ready to learn and try new things.

A resume of your time at ALU so far.

I have had the opportunity to work a peer mentor and an admissions intern at ALU.

Ideas for the future & careers, for each student.

I hope to specialise in Software Engineering and Artificial Intelligence in the health care field.
